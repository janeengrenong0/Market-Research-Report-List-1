<p><p>The biosimilars treatment market consists of three main types: recombinant non-glycosylated proteins, recombinant glycosylated proteins, and recombinant peptides. Recombinant non-glycosylated proteins, such as insulin, lack sugar chains and are simpler in structure. Recombinant glycosylated proteins, like monoclonal antibodies, have sugar modifications that influence their efficacy and stability. Recombinant peptides are short chains of amino acids, often used for targeted therapies. Each type serves different therapeutic needs, reflecting advancements in biologic drug development and manufacturing techniques.</p></p>
